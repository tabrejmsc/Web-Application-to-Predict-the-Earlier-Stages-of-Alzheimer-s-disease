An Ensemble-Learning Based Web Application to Predict the Earlier Stages of Alzheimer’s disease

Background
The Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) provides a pool of well-established biomarkers namely Aβ42, tau, p-tau that can detect the earlier stages of Alzheimer's Disease (AD) with an acceptable level of accuracy. Therefore we have utilized a proteomic screen of Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) from well-characterized Mild Cognitive Impaired (MCI) and cognitively normal subjects to look for a novel set of protein biomarkers that can predict the earlier stages of AD with greater accuracy than Aβ42, tau, and p-tau proteins.
Methods and Findings
Person Correlation Coefficient was used to filter out features having a Correlation Coefficient > 0.99. Recursive Feature Elimination (RFE) method utilizing a cost-sensitive Linear Support Vector Machine (SVM) was used to produce a set of three most informative features (Cystatin C, MMP10, and tau proteins). Mean levels of Cystatin C, MMP10, and tau proteins were found to be significant at 95 % Confidence Interval (CI) between cognitive normal (CDR 0) and cognitively impaired groups (CDR ≥ 0.5). An ensemble model was created using the three most informative features by averaging the output probability of the two best performing classifier based predictive models (Logistic regression and Linear SVM). The ROC_AUC, PR_AUC, and Sensitivity results at 80 % specificity showed that the ensemble model performed significantly better at 95 % CI in discriminating CDR 0 from CDR > 0 subjects than similar predictive model generated using a conventional set of CSF protein biomarkers (tau, p_tau, and Ab_42). A web application of our ensemble model to detect the earlier stages of AD was generated using Flask and was later deployed on Heroku at https://alzpredict.herokuapp.com.
Conclusions
Our online ensemble-based prediction system is one of its kind for predicting the early stages of AD using CSF protein biomarkers.
Keywords: Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI), Alzheimer's disease (AD), Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) protein biomarkers, Recursive Feature Elimination method, ensemble-learning model, online prediction system.
